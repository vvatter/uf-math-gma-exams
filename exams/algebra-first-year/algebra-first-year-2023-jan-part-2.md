# Algebra, first year exam, January 2023, Part 2

*Answer 4 questions. Write your solutions clearly in complete English sentences. You may quote results (within reason) as long as you state them clearly.*

**1.** Prove that the following polynomials are irreducible in \(\mathbb{Q}[x]\), stating clearly any theorems you wish to apply.
* (a) \(x^3-x^2+x+5\).
* (b) \(x^6-300\).
* (c) \(x^4-4x^3+2x^2+21x-7\).

**2.** Show that the ring \(\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-2}]\) is a Euclidean domain with respect to the norm \(N(a+b\sqrt{-2})=a^2+2b^2\). (You may regard this ring as a subring of the field of complex numbers, with \(\sqrt{-2}=i\sqrt{2}\).)

**3.** Let \(R\) and \(S\) be commutative rings with 1 and \(\phi:R\to S\) a ring homomorphism with \(\phi(1)=1\). Let \(I\) be an ideal of \(S\) and \(\phi^{-1}(I)=\{r\in R\mid \phi(r)\in I\}\).
* (a) Show that \(\phi^{-1}(I)\) is an ideal of \(R\).
* (b) Show that if \(I\) is a prime ideal then so is \(\phi^{-1}(I)\).
* (c) If \(I\) is maximal, is \(\phi^{-1}(I)\) necessarily maximal? Give a proof or counterexample.

**4.** Let \(R\) be a ring with 1 and \(M\) a (unital) left \(R\)-module.
* (a) State what it means for \(M\) to satisfy the Ascending Chain Condition (i.e. for \(M\) to be Noetherian).
* (b) Prove that if \(M\) satisfies the ACC then \(M\) is finitely generated.
* (c) Give an example of a finitely generated \(R\)-module \(M\) that does not satisfy the ACC.

**5.** Use the rational canonical form to find one representative of each conjugacy class of elements of order 6 in \(\mathrm{GL}(4,\mathbb{Q})\).

**6.** Let \(K\subset F\subset E\) be fields.
* (a) If \(|F:K|=n\) and \(|E:F|=m\), where \(m\) and \(n\) are finite, show that \(|E:K|=nm\).
* (b) Prove that if \(|E:K|\) is finite, then \(|F:K|\) and \(|E:F|\) are both finite.
