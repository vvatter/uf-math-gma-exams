# Algebra, first year exam, January 2014, Part 1

*Answer four problems. (If you turn in more, the first four will be graded.) Within reason, you may quote theorems as long as you state them clearly.*

**1.** Give an example of each of the following.
* (a) A non-trivial abelian group.
* (b) A non-abelian simple group.
* (c) A non-nilpotent solvable group.
* (d) A non-abelian nilpotent group.

**2.** Let \(G\) be a group and let \(g \in G\).
* (a) Define what is meant by the order of \(g\).
* (b) Let \(g\) have infinite order, and suppose that \(N\) is a finite normal subgroup of \(G\). Prove, from your definition, that the element \(gN \in G/N\) has infinite order.

**3.** This problem has two parts.
* (a) Define what it means for a subset of a group \(G\) to generate \(G\).
* (b) Let \(G\) be a group, and suppose \(|G| = 2^n\). Prove that there is a subset \(S\) of cardinality \(n\) such that \(S\) generates \(G\).

**4.** State and prove the Second Isomorphism Theorem for groups.

**5.** This problem has two parts.
* (a) Let \(S\) be a simple group and suppose that \(S\) acts non-trivially on a finite set \(A\) with \(|A| = n\). Prove that \(|S| \leq n!\).
* (b) Suppose \(G = A_5\) (the alternating group on five letters) is acting on a set \(A\) in such a way that no element of \(A\) is fixed by every element of \(G\). Suppose that \(|A| = 10\). Prove that \(G\) has at most two orbits in its action on \(A\).

**6.** State and prove the First Sylow Theorem.
