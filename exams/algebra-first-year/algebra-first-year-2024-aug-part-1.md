# Algebra, first year exam, August 2024, Part 1

*Answer four problems. (If you turn in more, the first four will be graded.) Within reason, you may quote theorems as long as you state them clearly.*

**1.** Give an example of each, with a few words of explanation.
* (a) A finite abelian simple group.
* (b) A nonabelian simple group.
* (c) A finite nilpotent group which is not a \(p\)-group.
* (d) A finite group which is solvable but not nilpotent.

**2.** Consider the symmetric group \(S_6\), and the corresponding alternating group \(A_6\).
* (a) How many elements of order 4 are there in \(S_6\)? Justify your answer.
* (b) How many elements of order 4 are there in \(A_6\)? Justify your answer.

**3.** Let \(G\) be a finite \(p\)-group and suppose that \(G\) acts on a finite set \(S\). Denote by \(S^G\) the set of all elements of \(S\) fixed by every element of \(G\). Show that \(|S| \equiv |S^G| \pmod p\), where \(|A|\) denotes the cardinality of the set \(A\).

**4.** Let \(G\) be a group of order \(32 \cdot 11 \cdot 41\).
* (a) Show that every 41-Sylow subgroup of \(G\) is normal.
* (b) Show that if \(H\) is a group of order \(32 \cdot 11\) then every 11-Sylow subgroup of \(H\) is normal.
* (c) Show that \(G\) has a normal cyclic subgroup of order \(451 = 11 \cdot 41\).

**5.** Let \(G\) be a group and let \(g \in G\).
* (a) Define what is meant by the order of \(g\).
* (b) Suppose that \(g\) has infinite order, and suppose that \(N\) is a finite normal subgroup of \(G\). Prove, from your definition, that the element \(gN \in G/N\) has infinite order.
* (c) Suppose that \(g\) has finite order, suppose that \(N\) is a finite normal subgroup of \(G\), and suppose that \(|N|\) is relatively prime to the order of \(g\). Prove, from your definition, that the element \(gN \in G/N\) has the same order as the element \(g \in G\).

**6.** Show that for any \(n \geq 5\), the alternating group \(A_n\) is the only nontrivial proper normal subgroup of the symmetric group \(S_n\).
