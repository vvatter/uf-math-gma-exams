# Algebra first year exam, May 2016, Part 1

*Answer four problems. (If you turn in more, the first four will be graded.) Within reason, you may quote theorems as long as you state them clearly.*

**1.** Give an example of each of the following, with some explanation.
* (a) A non-solvable finite group.
* (b) A non-cyclic infinite abelian group.
* (c) A non-abelian solvable finite group.

**2.** Let \(G\) be a finite group and suppose \(G\) acts transitively on a set \(\Omega\). Prove that \(|\Omega|\) (the number of elements in \(\Omega\)) is finite and that \(|\Omega|\) divides \(|G|\).

**3.** Let \(G\) be a group, and let \(A\) and \(B\) be normal subgroups of \(G\) such that \(A \cap B = 1\). Prove that every element of \(A\) commutes with every element of \(B\).

**4.** Let \(G\) be a group of order \(14\) and let \(H\) be a (multiplicative) cyclic group of order \(4\). Suppose that \(\phi:G\to H\) is a group homomorphism. Let \(g\in G\). Prove that there exists some \(h\in H\) such that \(\phi(g)=h^2\).

**5.** Let \(a=(1\ 2\ 3\ 4)(5\ 6)(7\ 8)\) and let \(b=(7\ 2\ 8\ 4)(1\ 6)(3\ 5)\) be elements of the symmetric group \(S_8\). Is there an element \(\sigma\in S_8\) such that \(\sigma a\sigma^{-1}=b\)? If so find one.

**6.** Prove that if \(G\) is a group of order \(56\), then \(G\) has a normal Sylow \(2\)-subgroup or a normal Sylow \(7\)-subgroup.
