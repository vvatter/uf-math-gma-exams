# Algebra first year exam, January 2026, Part 2

*Answer 4 questions. Write your solutions clearly in complete English sentences. You may quote theorems (within reason) as long as you state them clearly.*

**1.** Prove that the following polynomials are irreducible in \(\mathbb{Q}[x]\), stating clearly any theorems you wish to apply.
* (a) \(x^4-2x^3+6x^2-x+5\)
* (b) \(x^5-4x+2\)
* (c) \(x^4+x^3+x^2+x+1\)

**2.** Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with \(1\ne 0\).
* (a) Define what it means for an element \(r\in R\) to be nilpotent.
* (b) Prove that the set of all nilpotent elements of \(R\) forms an ideal (called the nilradical of \(R\)).
* (c) Show that the nilradical is contained in every prime ideal of \(R\).

**3.** Let \(R=\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-3}]=\{a+b\sqrt{-3}\mid a,b\in\mathbb{Z}\}\).
* (a) Show that the function \(N(a+b\sqrt{-3})=a^2+3b^2\) is multiplicative, that is, \(N(rs)=N(r)N(s)\) for all \(r,s\in R\).
* (b) Prove that \(R\) is not a unique factorization domain.
* (c) Is \(R\) a principal ideal domain? Justify your answer.

**4.** Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with 1 and let \(M\) be a module over \(R\).
* (a) State what it means for \(M\) to be a Noetherian \(R\)-module, and for \(R\) to be a Noetherian ring.
* (b) Prove that if \(M\) is a Noetherian \(R\)-module, then every nonempty set of submodules has a maximal element.
* (c) Give an example of a non-Noetherian commutative ring with 1.

**5.** Using the rational canonical form, find one representative of each conjugacy class of elements of order 3 in \(\mathrm{GL}(4,\mathbb{Q})\).

**6.** Let \(K\subseteq E\) be fields.
* (a) Define what it means for an element of \(E\) to be algebraic over \(K\).
* (b) Prove that if \(\alpha,\beta\in E\) are algebraic over \(K\), then \(K(\alpha,\beta)\) is finite-dimensional over \(K\).
* (c) Deduce that the elements of \(E\) that are algebraic over \(K\) form a field.
