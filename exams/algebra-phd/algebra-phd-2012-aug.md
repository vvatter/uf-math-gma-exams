# Algebra, PhD exam, August 2012

*Answer seven problems. (If you turn in more, the first seven will be graded.) Within reason, you may quote theorems as long as you state them clearly.*

**1.** This problem has two parts.
* (a) Show that for all \(n \geq 0\) there exists an irreducible polynomial \(f \in \mathbb{Q}[x]\) of degree greater than \(n\) that does not satisfy the hypotheses of Eisenstein’s criterion.
* (b) Give an example, with proofs, of an irreducible polynomial which is not separable.

**2.** Let \(n \geq 1\) and let \(F\) be a splitting field of \(x^n-1 \in \mathbb{Q}[x]\).
* (a) Prove that the Galois group of \(F\) over \(\mathbb{Q}\) is abelian.
* (b) When \(n=8\) show that the Galois group is of order \(4\) and not cyclic.

**3.** Prove that the Galois group of \(x^5-4x+2 \in \mathbb{Q}[x]\) is the symmetric group of degree \(5\).

**4.** Let \(F\) be a free group on a finite set \(X\) of size \(n\). Prove that the quotient of \(F\) by its commutator subgroup is isomorphic to \(\mathbb{Z}^n\).

**5.** Let \(R\) be a ring with \(1\) and let
\[
0 \to A \xrightarrow{f} B \xrightarrow{g} C \to 0
\]
be a short exact sequence of (left) \(R\)-modules. Show that for any (right) \(R\)-module \(M\), the sequence
\[
M \otimes_R A \xrightarrow{1^M \otimes f} M \otimes_R B \xrightarrow{1^M \otimes g} M \otimes_R C \to 0
\]
of tensor products, is exact.

**6.** Let \(F\) be a field and \(F[[x]]\) the ring of formal power series in the indeterminate \(x\).
* (a) Prove that \(F[[x]]\) is a principal ideal domain.
* (b) Determine all of the prime ideals of \(F[[x]]\).
* (c) Is \(F[[x]]\) Noetherian? Artinian?

**7.** All parts involve ideals in a commutative ring with identity. Let \(S\) be a multiplicative set in \(R\).
* (a) Prove that if \(I\) is an ideal of \(R\) then \(S^{-1}I=\left\{\frac{a}{s}\mid a\in I, s\in S\right\}\) is an ideal in the ring \(S^{-1}R\), the localization of \(R\) with respect to \(S\).
* (b) Define the radical \(\operatorname{Rad} I\) of an ideal \(I\) and show that it is an ideal.
* (c) Prove that \(S^{-1}(\operatorname{Rad} I)=\operatorname{Rad}(S^{-1}I)\).

**8.** You may not answer this problem just by quoting Nakayama’s Lemma. Let \(R\) be a commutative local ring with \(1\) and \(J\) its unique maximal ideal.
* (a) Prove that \(1-a\) is a unit for every element \(a\) of \(J\),
* (b) Let \(M\) be an \(R\)-module, with generators \(x_1,x_2,x_3\) and suppose that \(x_2\) and \(x_3\) lie in \(JM\). Show that \(M=Rx_1\).

**9.** Let \(R\) be a ring with \(1\). All modules are unitary.
* (a) Define the concept of a projective \(R\)-module.
* (b) Show that if \(P\) is a projective \(R\)-module, then there is an \(R\)-module \(K\) such that \(P\oplus K\) is isomorphic to a free module.
* (c) Show that for every nonzero element \(x\) of a free \(\mathbb{Z}\)-module \(F\) there is a homomorphism from \(F\) to a finite group such that the image of \(x\) is nontrivial.
* (d) Prove that \(\mathbb{Q}\) is not a projective \(\mathbb{Z}\)-module. (Hint: Show that \(\mathbb{Q}\) has no nontrivial finite quotients. Then use the earlier parts.)

**10.** Let \(D\) be a division ring with center \(K\) and suppose \(a\) and \(b\) are elements of \(D\) which are algebraic over \(K\) and have the same minimum polynomial. Prove that \(b=dad^{-1}\) for some nonzero element \(d\in D\).

**11.** Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category and let \((A_i)_{i\in I}\) be a family of objects of \(\mathcal{C}\).
* (a) Define what is a coproduct of \((A_i)_{i\in I}\) in \(\mathcal{C}\).
* (b) Prove that in the category of sets the coproduct of two sets is their disjoint union.
