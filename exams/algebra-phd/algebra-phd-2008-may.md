# Algebra PhD exam, May 2008

*Answer seven problems. Write your answers clearly in complete English sentences. You may quote results (within reason) as long as you state them clearly.*

**1.** Let \(n>2\) and let \(\zeta\) be a primitive \(n\)-th root of unity over \(\mathbb Q\) (the field of rational numbers). Prove that
\[
[\mathbb Q(\zeta+\zeta^{-1}):\mathbb Q]=\phi(n)/2.
\]

**2.** Calculate the Galois group of \(x^5-9x+3\) over \(\mathbb Q\), the field of rational numbers. Justify your answer carefully.

**3.** Prove that the group defined by generators \(a\) and \(b\) and relations \(a^2=b^3=1\), \(abab=1\) is isomorphic to \(S_3\).

**4.** Let \(A\) be a torsion abelian group. Calculate \(A\otimes_{\mathbb Z}\mathbb Q\), where \(\mathbb Q\) denotes the additive group of the rational numbers.

**5.** Define projective module. Prove from your definition that a module is projective if and only if it is a direct summand of a free module.

**6.** State and prove Hilbert’s Nullstellensatz.

**7.** Let \(R\) be an integral domain. Define what it means for a fractional ideal to be invertible. Prove, from your definition, that if \(I\) is an invertible fractional ideal of \(R\), then \(I\) is finitely generated.

**8.** Prove the following version of the Short Five Lemma. Let \(R\) be a ring and let the following diagram of left \(R\)-modules
\[
\require{amscd}
\begin{CD}
0 @>>> A @>{f}>> B @>{g}>> C @>>> 0 \\
@. @V{\alpha}VV @V{\beta}VV @V{\gamma}VV @. \\
0 @>>> A' @>{f'}>> B' @>{g'}>> C' @>>> 0
\end{CD}
\]
be a commutative diagram whose rows are short exact sequences. Suppose that both \(\alpha\) and \(\gamma\) are isomorphisms. Prove that \(\beta\) is an isomorphism.

**9.** Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with \(1_R\). Prove that \(R\) is a local ring if and only if for all \(r,s\in R\) such that \(r+s=1_R\), either \(r\) or \(s\) is a unit.

**10.** Determine up to isomorphism all semisimple noncommutative rings with \(512=2^9\) elements.

**11.** Let \(K\) be a field and let \(A\) be a central simple algebra over \(K\) such that \(\dim_K(A)=n\) is finite. Prove that \(A\otimes_K A^{\mathrm{op}}\cong \mathrm{M}_{n\times n}(K)\).
