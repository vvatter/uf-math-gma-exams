# Algebra PhD exam, August 2018

*Answer seven problems. Write your answers clearly in complete English sentences. You may quote results (within reason) as long as you state them clearly.*

**1.** Determine the Galois group of the polynomial
\[
f=x^5-2\in\mathbb{Q}[x].
\]

**2.** Let \(F\) be a field of characteristic zero and \(E\) a finite extension field of \(F\). Prove that \(E\) is a simple extension of \(F\), i.e. there exists \(\alpha\in E\) such that \(E=F(\alpha)\).

**3.** Let \(R\) be a ring with 1. Prove the equivalence of the following conditions.
* (a) Every unital left \(R\)-module is projective.
* (b) Every short exact sequence of unital left \(R\)-modules splits.
* (c) Every unital left \(R\)-module is injective.

**4.** Prove that the additive group of the field of rational numbers is not a projective \(\mathbb{Z}\)-module.

**5.** Let \(R\) and \(S\) be rings, \(A\) a right \(R\)-module, \(B\) an \((R,S)\)-bimodule and \(C\) a left \(S\)-module.
* (a) Explain how \(B\otimes_S C\) is a left \(R\)-module and \(A\otimes_R B\) is a right \(S\)-module.
* (b) Prove that we have an isomorphism (of abelian groups)
  \[
  A\otimes_R(B\otimes_S C)\cong(A\otimes_R B)\otimes_S C.
  \]

**6.** Let \(R\) be an integral domain and for each maximal ideal \(M\), regard the localization \(R_M\) as a subring of the field of fractions \(F\) of \(R\). Show that \(\bigcap_M R_M=R\). (Hint: for \(x\in F\), consider the ideal \(D=\{a\in R\mid ax\in R\}\). If \(x\in R_M\) what does it say about \(D\)?)

**7.** Let \(R\) be a Dedekind domain and \(I\) a nonzero ideal of \(R\).
* (a) Prove that \(R/I\) is Artinian.
* (b) Prove that if \(R\) is itself Artinian, then it is a field.

**8.** Let \(F[[X]]\) denote the ring of formal power series over the field \(F\).
* (a) Prove that \(a_0+a_1X+a_2X^2+\cdots\in F[[X]]\) is a unit if and only if \(a_0\ne0\).
* (b) Prove that \(F[[X]]\) is a discrete valuation ring.

**9.** Let \(R\) and \(S\) be commutative rings with 1. Prove the Lying-Over Theorem: Let \(S\) be an integral extension of \(R\) and \(P\) a prime ideal of \(R\). Then there exists a prime ideal \(Q\) of \(S\) such that \(Q\cap R=P\).

**10.** Prove that the ring of \(n\times n\) matrices with entries in a field \(F\) is a simple ring when \(n\ge1\). Describe explicitly one of its minimal left ideals.

**11.** Let \(\mathcal C\) be a category and let \(\Lambda\) be a set. Suppose that for each \(\lambda\in\Lambda\) we are given an object \(X_\lambda\) in \(\mathcal C\).
* (a) Give the definition of a product of the collection of objects \(\{X_\lambda\}_{\lambda\in\Lambda}\).
* (b) Prove that any two products of the \(\{X_\lambda\}_{\lambda\in\Lambda}\) are isomorphic.
* (c) Prove that in the category of Groups a product exists for any collection of objects indexed by a set \(\Lambda\).
* (d) Prove that if, in (c), the groups in the collection happen to be abelian, then a product as in (c) is also a product in the category of Abelian groups.
