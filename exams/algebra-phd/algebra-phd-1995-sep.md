# Algebra, PhD exam, September 1995

*Answer seven problems. Write your answers clearly in complete English sentences. You may quote results (within reason) as long as you state them clearly.*

**1.** This problem has two parts.
* (a) Give the definitions of a solvable group and a nilpotent group.
* (b) Let \(G\) be a finite solvable group. Let \(F(G)\) be the largest normal nilpotent subgroup of \(G\). Prove that if \(G\) is not abelian, then \(F(G)>Z(G)\), where \(Z(G)\) is the center of \(G\) (ie., \(F(G)\) is bigger but not equal to \(Z(G)\).

**2.** A proper subgroup \(M\) of a group \(G\) is maximal if whenever \(M<H\leq G\), we have \(H=M\) or \(H=G\). Suppose that \(G\) is a finite group has only one maximal subgroup. Prove that \(G\) is cyclic of prime power order.

**3.** Let \(A\) be a commutative Artinian ring with identity. Prove the following statements.
* (a) All prime ideals are maximal.
* (b) There is only finite number of prime, or maximal ideals.
* (c) The ideal \(N\) of nilpotent elements in \(A\) is a nilpotent ideal.

**4.** A module \(F\) over a commutative ring with identity is called flat if for any short exact sequence \(0\longrightarrow E'\longrightarrow E\longrightarrow E''\longrightarrow 0\), the sequence \(0\longrightarrow F\otimes E'\longrightarrow F\otimes E\longrightarrow F\otimes E''\longrightarrow 0\) is exact.
* (a) Give an example of a non-flat module.
* (b) Let \(0\longrightarrow F'\longrightarrow F\longrightarrow F''\longrightarrow 0\) be an exact sequence of modules over a commutative ring with identity. Suppose \(F''\) is a flat module. Prove that \(F'\) is flat if and only if \(F\) is flat.

**5.** This problem has two parts.
* (a) State the Jacobson Density Theorem.
* (b) Using Jacobson’s density Theorem to prove the following Wedderburn’s Theorem: Let \(R\) be a ring, and \(E\) a simple, faithful module over \(R\). Let \(D=\operatorname{End}_R(E)\), and assume that \(E\) is a finite dimension over \(D\). Then \(R=\operatorname{End}_D(E)\).

**6.** Let \(A\) be a commutative ring with identity, let \(M\) be an \(A\)-module, and let \(N\) be a submodule of \(M\).
* (a) Define what it means for \(M\) to be Noetherian.
* (b) Prove that if \(M\) is Noetherian then the quotient \(M/N\) must also be Noetherian.
* (c) Prove that a submodule of a Noetherian module is Noetherian.

**7.** Let \(A\) be a commutative ring with identity. Let \(M\) be a \(A\)-module.
* (a) Give the definition of an associated prime of \(M\).
* (b) Prove that if \(A\) is Noetherian and \(M\neq 0\), then \(M\) has an associated prime.

**8.** Let \(p\) be a prime and let \(U_p\) be the subgroup of the group of complex roots of unity consisting of those roots of unity whose order is a power of \(p\).
* (a) Show that the map \(x\longmapsto x^n\) is a surjective endomorphism of \(U_p\), which is an automorphism if and only if \(p\) does not divide \(n\).
* (b) Let \(L\) denote the localization of the integers at the prime \(p\). Deduce from (a) that \(U_p\) is a \(L\)-module for which \((p)U_p=U_p\).
* (c) State Nakayama’s Lemma and explain why the module \(U_p\) is not a counterexample.

**9.** Consider the polynomial over the field \(F_2\) with two elements:
\[g(x)=x^4+x^3+x^2+x+1\]
* (a) Prove that \(g(x)\) is irreducible over \(F_2\).
* (b) Let \(K\) be a splitting field for \(g(x)\) over \(F_2\) and let \(r\in K\) be a root of \(g(x)\). Factor \(g(x)\) into irreducibles over \(F_2(r)\).
* (c) Show that \(K=F_2(r)\).
* (d) Find the Galois group \(\operatorname{Gal}(K/F_2)\).

**10.** Let \(K/k\) be a cyclic extension of fields with finite Galois group \(\langle\sigma\rangle\).
* (a) State the Hilbert 90 theorem (the multiplicative or the additive form).
* (b) Suppose \([K:k]=n\) is prime to the \(\operatorname{Char}(k)\) and \(k\) contains a primitive \(n\)-th root of unity. Prove that there exists \(\alpha\in K\) such that \(K=k(\alpha)\) and \(\operatorname{Irr}(\alpha,k,x)\) (i.e., the irreducible polynomial of \(\alpha\) over \(k\)) \(=x^n-a\) for some \(a\in k\).

**11.** Let \(G=\langle g\rangle\) be a finite cyclic group and regard \(\mathbb{Z}\) (the integers) as a trivial module of \(G\) (i.e., \(\mathbb{Z}[G]\)-module). Compute for \(n\geq 1\), \(H^n(G,\mathbb{Z})\). Justify your answer.
