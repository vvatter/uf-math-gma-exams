# Analysis, PhD exam, May 1999

*State the following theorems in problems 1–8. Prove one of the theorems in problem 3 or 7. Solve problems 9–13, where \((X,\Sigma,\mu)\) is a measure space and \(F\) is a Banach space. Write complete computations and explanations. Do not use arrows or other unnecessary signs. Use words for explanations. Write complete sentences.*

**1.** Hahn–Banach

**2.** The Egorov theorem

**3.** The Vitali convergence theorem

**4.** The Radon–Nikodym theorem

**5.** The Fubini theorem

**6.** The monotone convergence theorem

**7.** The completeness theorem for \(L_F^2(\mu)\).

**8.** The equality \(|g\mu|=|g|\mu\).

**9.** If \(f_n\to f\) in \(L_F^2(\mu)\) and \(f_n\to g\), \(\mu\)-a.e., then \(f=g\), \(\mu\)-a.e.

**10.** Let \(\mathcal F\subset\Sigma\) be a σ-algebra. Prove the existence of the conditional expectation \(E(f/\mathcal F)\) for \(f\in L_F^2(\mu)\).

**11.** Let \(\mathcal R\) be a ring generating \(\Sigma\) and \(f\in L_F^1(\mu)\). Prove that if \(\int_A f\,d\mu=0\) for every \(A\in\mathcal R\), then \(f=0\), \(\mu\)-a.e.

**12.** Let \((X,\mathcal F)\) and \((Y,\mathcal J)\) be measurable spaces, \(\mu:\mathcal F\to\mathbb R\) and \(\nu:\mathcal J\to\mathbb R\), σ-additive measures. Prove the existence of the product-measure \(\mu\times\nu:\mathcal F\times\mathcal J\to\mathbb R\) satisfying \(\mu\times\nu(A\times B)=\mu(A)\nu(B)\) for \(A\times B\in\mathcal F\times\mathcal J\) and \(|\mu\times\nu|=|\mu|\times|\nu|\).
