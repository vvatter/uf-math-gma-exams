# Analysis, PhD exam, October 1994

*\((X,\Sigma,\mu)\) is a finite measure space. State the theorems in Problems 1–10. Prove the assertion in Problem 11. Solve Problems 12–15.*

**1.** Lebesgue dominated convergence theorem

**2.** Fatou lemma

**3.** Vitali theorem

**4.** Monotone convergence theorem

**5.** The Radon–Nikodym theorem

**6.** The Egorov theorem

**7.** The Fubini theorem

**8.** The Lebesgue decomposition theorem

**9.** The uniform boundedness theorem

**10.** The Hahn–Banach theorem

**11.** \((L^1)^*=L^\infty\)

**12.** Find \(\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}\int_0^1 nx^n\,dx\). (Hint: use the monotone convergence theorem.)

**13.** Let \(\mu\) be the Lebesgue measure on \(\mathbb{R}\) and \(f\) a Lebesgue-integrable function on \(\mathbb{R}\), such that \(\int_0^x f\,d\mu=0\) for every \(x\in\mathbb{R}\). What can you say about \(f\)?

**14.** Let \(f\ge 0\) be \(\mu\)-integrable such that \(\int f\,d\mu\le 1\). Prove the \(\int \frac{1}{f}\,d\mu\ge 1\). (Hint, use the Schwarz inequality for \(\sqrt f\) and \(\frac{1}{\sqrt f}\).)

**15.** Let \(R\) be a ring of subsets of \(X\), \(E\) a Banach space and \(\mu:R\to E\) be a finitely additive measure. Prove that \(\mu\) is \(\sigma\)-additive iff for any decreasing sequence \((A_n)\) from \(R\) with \(\bigcap_n A_n=\phi\) we have \(\lim \mu(A_n)=0\).
