# Analysis, PhD exam, May 2009

**1.** State and prove the Fubini theorems.

**2.** This problem has three parts.
* (a) State the Vitali integral convergence theorem.
* (b) State the Lebesgue Dominated Convergence Theorem.
* (c) Use (a) to prove (b) [the convergence in measure version].

**3.** This problem has two parts.
* (a) Let \(\mu:\Sigma\to[-\infty,\infty]\) be countably additive, where \(\Sigma\) is a \(\sigma\)-algebra. Define positive and negative sets for \(\mu\) and state the Hahn decomposition theorem for \(\mu\).
* (b) Let \(\mu\) be as above. Does there exist a set \(E\in\Sigma\) such that \(\mu(E)\) is the minimum value for \(\mu\) on \(\Sigma\)? Prove.

**4.** Suppose \(\{f_n\}\) is a sequence of integrable functions with the property that \(\sum \int |f_n|\,d\mu<\infty\). Analyze the pointwise convergence of \(\sum f_n(x)\), for \(x\in X\).

**5.** Let \(Y\) be an orthonormal set in a Hilbert space and suppose \(f:Y\to\mathbb C\) is given. State necessary and sufficient conditions for \(\sum_{y\in Y}f(y)y\) to converge. Prove.

**6.** Let \(X\) be a normed space and \(\pi:X\to X^{**}\) the natural map. Under what conditions is \(\pi(X)\) closed in \(X^{**}\) (equipped with the norm topology)? Prove.

**7.** This problem has two parts.
* (a) State the Principle of Uniform Boundedness for a family of operators.
* (b) Let \(X\) be a normed space. Use (a) to show that if \(A\subset X\) satisfies \(\sup_{a\in A}|x^*a|<\infty\) for each \(x^*\in X^*\), then \(\sup_{a\in A}|a|<\infty\).

**8.** Let \(X\) be a normed space, and assume \(M\) is a closed linear subspace of \(X\). Let \(z\in X\setminus M\) and \(S=\operatorname{span}\{z,M\}\). Show \(S\) is closed in \(X\). [Hint: Let \(f:S\to\mathbb C\) be defined by \(f(x+\alpha z)=\alpha\), for \(x\in M\) and \(\alpha\in\mathbb C\). Show \(\|f\|\le 1/\operatorname{dist}(z,M)\), hence \(f\in S^*\).]
