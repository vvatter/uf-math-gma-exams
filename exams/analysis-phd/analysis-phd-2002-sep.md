# Analysis, PhD exam, September 2002

*State the following theorems (Problems 1–5). State and prove one of the following two theorems (Problems 6–7). Solve the following problems (Problems 8–12). For Problems 8–11, let \((X,\Sigma,\mu)\) be a measure space and \(E\) a Banach space. Write complete computations and explanations. Do not use arrows or other unnecessary signs. Use words for explanations. Write complete sentences. Write complete statements of theorems (including the framework).*

**1.** The Egorov theorem.

**2.** The Lebesgue theorem.

**3.** The dual of \(L^p(\mu)\), \(1\leq p<\infty\).

**4.** The Fubini theorem for \(\mu\times\nu\)-integrable functions.

**5.** The Riesz representation theorem (of linear continuous functional on the space \(C(K)\) of continuous functions on a compact space \(K\)).

**6.** The representation of the dual of \(L^1(\mu)\).

**7.** The Fubini theorem for \(\mathcal S\times\mathcal T\)-measurable functions.

**8.** Assume \(\mu\) is finite. Let \(\mathcal F\subset\Sigma\) be a sub-σ-algebra. Prove the existence of the conditional expectation \(E(f\mid\mathcal F)\) for functions \(f\in L_E^1(\mu)\).

**9.** Let \(\mathcal R\subset\Sigma\) be an algebra generating \(\Sigma\) and \(f\in L_E^1(\mu)\). Prove that there is a sequence \((f_n)\) of \(\mathcal R\)-step functions converging to \(f\), \(\mu\)-a.e., and in the mean.

**10.** Let \(\mathcal R\subset\Sigma\) be an algebra generating \(\Sigma\) and \(f\in L_E^1(\mu)\). Prove that if \(\int_A f\,d\mu=0\) for every \(A\in\mathcal R\), then \(f=0\), \(\mu\)-a.e.

**11.** Assume \(\mu\) is finite and let \(f_n\in L_E^1(\mu)\), \(n=1,2,\ldots\). Prove that if \(f_n\to f\) uniformly, then \(f\in L_E^1(\mu)\) and \(f_n\to f\) in the mean.
