# Logic, PhD exam, September 2000

*Answer 7 questions, including at least one from each of the four segments. Problems 1–3 concern General Logic; problems 4–5 concern Model Theory; problems 6–8 concern Set Theory; problems 9–11 concern Recursion Theory.*

**1.** State Gödel’s incompleteness theorem, and briefly sketch its proof.

**2.** Show that any complete, finitely axiomatizable first-order theory is decidable.

**3.** Give an example of two countable models which are elementarily equivalent, but not isomorphic.

**4.** State and sketch a proof of the completeness theorem.

**5.** State and sketch a proof of the Łoś theorem about ultraproducts of models.

**6.** Prove that a countable union of countable sets is countable.

**7.** Sketch the proof that \(L \models \mathrm{GCH}\).

**8.** Prove that if \(\kappa\) is a regular, uncountable cardinal then any diagonal intersection of closed and unbounded subsets of \(\kappa\) is closed and unbounded.

**9.** Show that any total function \(f:\omega\to\omega\) with a recursively enumerable graph is recursive. Show that this is not true for partial functions.

**10.** Explain why, for any \(A\subseteq\omega\), there are at most countably many sets \(B\) such that \(B<_T A\).

**11.** State and prove Kleene’s Recursion (or Fixed Point) Theorem.
