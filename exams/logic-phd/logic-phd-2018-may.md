# Logic, PhD exam, May 2018

*Answer six questions, at least one from each section: problems 1–3 are Section 1, problems 4–6 are Section 2, and problems 7–9 are Section 3.*

**1.** Provide a complete description of a formal proof system for first order logic.

**2.** Sketch the proof of the completeness theorem for first order logic.

**3.** State and prove Gödel’s diagonal lemma.

**4.** Sketch the proof that the Continuum Hypothesis holds in the constructible universe \(L\).

**5.** Show that Zorn’s lemma implies the well-ordering principle.

**6.** Sketch the proof of consistency of the Continuum Hypothesis using forcing.

**7.** Show that every nonempty \(\Sigma_1\)-definable set of natural numbers is the range of a total recursive function.

**8.** Sketch the proof of Friedberg–Muchnik theorem: there are two subsets of \(\omega\) which are incomparable in the sense of the Turing ordering.

**9.** Explain what a simple set is and provide an example with a proof.
