# Logic, PhD exam, May 2023

*Solve 5 problems of the following; at least one from problems 1–3, at least one from problems 4–6, and at least one from problems 7–9. Problems 1–3 concern Set Theory; problems 4–6 concern Computability; problems 7–9 concern Model theory.*

**1.** State Martin’s Axiom for \(\aleph_1\) and prove that it implies the negation of the Continuum Hypothesis.

**2.** Give the definition of a standard Borel space. Sketch the proof that any two uncountable standard Borel spaces are Borel isomorphic.

**3.** Let \(\kappa\) be an uncountable cardinal. Show that there is an ultrafilter on \(\kappa\) containing no sets of cardinality smaller than \(\kappa\).

**4.** State two different common definitions of the class of computable functions and sketch the proof that they give the same class.

**5.** Define the many-one reducibility and sketch the proof that there is a many-one degree strictly between \(0\) and \(0'\).

**6.** Prove that every infinite computably enumerable subset of the natural numbers contains an infinite computable subset.

**7.** What is quantifier elimination for theories? Give an example of a theory which has it, and prove that it has it. Give an example of theory which does not have it, and prove that it does not have it.

**8.** State the Łoś’ theorem and prove it.

**9.** Give an example of a relational Fraïssé class which has the disjoint amalgamation property, and an example of a relational Fraïssé class which does not have it.
