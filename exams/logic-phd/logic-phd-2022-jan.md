# Logic, PhD exam, January 2022

*Solve 5 problems of the following; at least one from each section. Problems 1–3 concern Set Theory. Problems 4–6 concern Computability. Problems 7–9 concern Model theory.*

**1.** Sketch the proof that the constructible universe satisfies the Continuum Hypothesis.

**2.** State Martin’s Axiom for a cardinal \(\kappa\). Show that it proves that the continuum has cardinality larger than \(\kappa\).

**3.** Given a Polish space \(X\), explain how the hyperspace \(K(X)\) of all compact subsets of \(X\) is topologized. Sketch the proof that Vietoris topology is Polish.

**4.** What are Turing degrees? Sketch the proof that for a Turing degree \(t\), the jump \(t'\) is of strictly larger Turing degree.

**5.** Provide at least two conceptually different definitions of the class of computable functions and show that they are equivalent.

**6.** What is Post’s problem? Provide the answer to the Post’s problem and an outline of the proof.

**7.** What is a Fraïssé class? State the Fraïssé theorem regarding the existence of a limit model for such a class.

**8.** What is quantifier elimination? Provide an example of a theory which has infinite models and quantifier elimination. Sketch a proof that the theory has quantifier elimination.

**9.** Find a complete theory with more than one countable model up to isomorphism. Provide two nonisomorphic countable models of the theory.
