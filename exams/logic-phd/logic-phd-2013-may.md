# Logic, PhD exam, May 2013

*Answer six questions and at least one from each section. Problems 1–3 are from Section 1, problems 4–6 are from Section 2, and problems 7–9 are from Section 3.*

**1.** Show that the theory of the class of infinite sets is complete and decidable, but is not finitely axiomatizable.

**2.** State and sketch a proof of the Łoś theorem about ultraproducts of models.

**3.** Show that for any elementary chain \(\{\mathcal A_i\}_{i\in\omega}\) of structures, \(\mathcal A_i\) is an elementary submodel of the union \(\mathcal A\).

**4.** State and prove the Schröder–Bernstein Theorem.

**5.** Show that Zorn’s Lemma implies the Well-Ordering Principle.

**6.** Show that for any notion of forcing \(\mathcal P\) and any countable set \(\mathcal D\) of \(\mathcal P\)-dense sets, there exists a \(\mathcal D\)-generic \(\mathcal P\)-filter.

**7.** Prove that if \(X\subseteq\omega\) has an infinite computably enumerable subset \(A\), then it has an infinite computable subset \(B\).

**8.** Sketch the proof that there are \(\le_m\)-incomparable c.e. sets.

**9.** Explain why the set \(WF\) of well-founded trees in \(\omega^*\) and the set \(WO\) of well-orderings of \(\omega\) are \(\Pi^1_1\) complete and prove the Boundedness Principle, that any \(\Sigma^1_1\) subset of \(WO\) is bounded below \(\omega_1\).
