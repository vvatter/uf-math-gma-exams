# Logic, PhD exam, August 2020

*Solve 5 problems of the following; at least one from each section.*

## A. Set Theory.

**1.** State Martin’s Axiom for \(\aleph_1\) and prove that it implies the negation of the Continuum Hypothesis.

**2.** State and prove Fodor’s pressing-down theorem.

**3.** Let \(\kappa\) be an uncountable cardinal. Show that there is an ultrafilter on \(\kappa\) containing no sets of cardinality smaller than \(\kappa\).

## B. Computability.

**1.** State two different common definitions of the class of computable functions and sketch the proof that they give the same class.

**2.** Define the many-one reducibility and sketch the proof that there is a many-one degree strictly between \(0\) and \(0'\).

**3.** Give an example of an infinite decidable structure and infinite undecidable structure and prove that they are such.

## C. Model theory.

**1.** What is quantifier elimination for theories? Give an example of a theory which has it, and prove that it has it.

**2.** State the Łoś’ theorem and prove it.

**3.** State and prove the Tarski criterion for elementarity of submodels.
