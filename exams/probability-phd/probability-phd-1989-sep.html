University of Florida, Department of Mathematics

Probability PhD exam, September 1989

  1. Let \(\{\overline{X}_m\}\) be a submartingale and \(T\) a stopping time such that \(E[T]<\infty\). Assume \(E[|\overline{X}_{m+1}-\overline{X}_m|\mid\mathcal F_m]\le M\) on \((T>m)\) for some constant \(M\). Show that \(\overline{X}_T\) is integrable and \(E[\overline{X}_0]\le E[\overline{X}_T]\).

  2. Let \(\overline{X}_m\to\overline{X}\) in probability. Let \(f\) be continuous on \(\mathbb R^1\). Then \(f(\overline{X}_m)\to f(\overline{X})\) in probability. Prove this.

  3. Can convergence a.e. be given by a metric? In other words, is there a metric \(\rho\) such that \(\rho(\overline{X}_m,0)\to0\) is equivalent to \(\overline{X}_m\to0\) a.e.? Substantiate.

  4. Explain the notion of convergence in distribution. Let \(\overline{X}_m\to\overline{X}\) in distribution. Does it follow that \(\overline{X}_m\to\overline{X}\) in probability? Suppose \(P[\overline{X}=a]=1\). Is it then true that \(\overline{X}_m\to\overline{X}\) in probability? Substantiate all your answers.

  5. Let \(\overline{Y}\in L^p\), \(|\overline{X}_m|\le\overline{Y}\), and \(\overline{X}_m\to\overline{X}\) in distribution. Show that \(E(|\overline{X}_m|^p)\to E(|\overline{X}|^p)\).

  6. Given that \(f\) is a characteristic function and \(|f(1/2)|=1\), what can you conclude?

  7. State and give a sketch of the proof of the strong law of large numbers.

  8. Let \(\overline{X}_m\) be i.i.d., \(E[\overline{X}_m]=0\), \(E[\overline{X}_m^2]=1\). Can this sequence be complete orthonormal? Substantiate.

  9. Let \(\mu\) be a non-trivial infinitely divisible distribution on \(\mathbb R^1\). Prove that \(\mu\) cannot have compact support.
