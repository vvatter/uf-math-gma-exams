# Analysis, first year exam, May 2016, Part 1

*Do exactly four problems. Work should be presented in a logical fashion in order to receive full credit.*

**1.** Let \(f\) be a continuous function on \([0,1]\). Prove that \(f\) is uniformly continuous.

**2.** Let \(\{G_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}\) be a family of open sets in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and \(\{F_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}\) a family of closed sets. Prove that \(\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}G_n\) is open and \(\bigcup_{n=1}^{N}F_n\) is closed for any finite \(N\). Give an example such that \(\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}F_n\) is not closed.

**3.** Suppose \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n\) is an absolutely convergent real series. Let \(b_n\) be a re-arrangement of \(a_n\). Prove that \(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}b_n=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n\).

**4.** Let \[f(x,y)=\begin{cases}\dfrac{xy}{x^2+y^2},&\text{if }(x,y)\ne(0,0),\\0,&\text{if }x=y=0.\end{cases}\] Prove that \(f\) is not continuous at \((0,0)\).

**5.** Suppose \(f'(x)>0\) for \(x\in(0,1)\) and let \(g\) be the inverse function of \(f\). Prove that for \(x_0\in(0,1)\), \(g'(f(x_0))=1/f'(x_0)\).
