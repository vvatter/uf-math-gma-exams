# Analysis, first year exam, January 2025, Part 2

*Answer FOUR questions in detail. State carefully any results used without proof.*

**1.** Let \(f:[0,1]\to\mathbb{R}\) be Riemann integrable. If \(f\) is continuous at \(0\), does it follow that
\[
\int_0^1 f(x^n)\,dx \to f(0) \quad\text{as } n\to\infty?
\]
If so, give a proof; if no, give a counterexample.

**2.** Let \((f_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}\) be a sequence of continuous real-valued functions on the metric space \(X\), in which \((x_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}\) is a convergent sequence.
* (i) Prove that if \((f_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}\) is uniformly convergent then \((f_n(x_n))_{n=0}^{\infty}\) converges.
* (ii) Show by example that \((f_n(x_n))_{n=0}^{\infty}\) can fail to converge if \((f_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}\) is merely pointwise convergent.

**3.** Let \(\Phi\) be an equicontinuous family of real-valued functions on the connected metric space \(X\). Prove that if \(\Phi\) is bounded at one point of \(X\) then \(\Phi\) is bounded at every point of \(X\). [Note: to say that \(\Phi\) is bounded at \(p\in X\) is to say that \(\{f(p):f\in\Phi\}\subseteq\mathbb{R}\) is bounded.]

**4.** Let \((f_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}\) be a sequence of measurable real-valued functions on some measure space. Prove that each of the following sets is measurable.
* (i) \(A=\{\omega:f_n(\omega)\to 0\text{ as }n\to\infty\}\).
* (ii) \(B=\{\omega:\sum_n f_n(\omega)\text{ converges absolutely}\}\).

**5.** This problem has two parts.
* (i) State the Monotone Convergence Theorem.
* (ii) On a measure space, let \((f_n)_{n=0}^{\infty}\) be a sequence of non-negative measurable functions converging pointwise to the function \(f\). Prove that if there exists a real number \(K\) such that each of the integrals \(\int f_n\,d\mu\) is at most \(K\), then \(f\) is integrable and \(\int f\,d\mu\leq K\).
