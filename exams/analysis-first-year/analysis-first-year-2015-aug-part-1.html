University of Florida, Department of Mathematics

Analysis first year exam, August 2015, Part 1

Do exactly 4 problems. Work must be presented in a neat and logical fashion in order to receive credit. Do not leave any gaps. When a theorem is used in a proof it must be precisely stated.

  1. Let \(E\) be a dense subset of a metric space \(X\) and let \(U\) be an open subset of \(X\). Prove that \(U \subset \overline{E \cap U}\). (Here \(\overline{E \cap U}\) denotes the closure of \(E \cap U\).)

  2. Let \(X\) be a metric space and \(K \subset X\) a compact subset. Prove that \(K\) is closed.

  3. Let \(X\) be a compact metric space and \(\mathcal F\) a collection of closed subsets of \(X\) with the following property: for all finite subcollections \(\mathcal G \subset \mathcal F\), the intersection \(\bigcap_{F \in \mathcal G} F\) is nonempty. Prove that \(\bigcap_{F \in \mathcal F} F\) is nonempty.

  4. Let \(X,Y\) be metric spaces and \(f:X\to Y\) a continuous function. Prove that if \(X\) is connected, then its image \(f(X)\) is connected.

  5. Let \((a_n)_{n\in\mathbb N}\) be a sequence of real numbers and \(\sigma\) a permutation of \(\mathbb N\). For each of the following provide either a proof or a counterexample.

    1. a)

      If the sequence \((a_n)_{n\in\mathbb N}\) converges then so does the rearrangement \((a_{\sigma(n)})_{n\in\mathbb N}\).

    2. b)

      If the series \(\sum_{n\in\mathbb N}a_n\) converges then so does the rearrangement \(\sum_{n\in\mathbb N}a_{\sigma(n)}\).

  6. Let \(f(x)=x^2\sin\frac{1}{x}\) for \(x\in\mathbb R\setminus\{0\}\) and \(f(0)=0\). Prove that \(f\) is differentiable at every \(x\in\mathbb R\). Is \(f'\) continuous at \(x=0\)? (Prove your claim.)
