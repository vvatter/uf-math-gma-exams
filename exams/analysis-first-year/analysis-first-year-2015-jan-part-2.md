# Analysis, first year exam, January 2015, Part 2

*Do exactly 2 of problems 1–3 (Part A) and 2 of problems 4–6 (Part B). Work must be presented in a neat and logical fashion in order to receive credit. Do not leave any gaps. When a theorem is used in a proof it must be precisely stated.*

**1.** Let \(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_nx^n\) be a power series with finite radius of convergence \(R\). Assume that the \(c_n\) and \(x\) are real valued. Let \(\epsilon>0\). State and prove the theorem concerning the uniform convergence of \(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_nx^n\) on \([-R+\epsilon,R-\epsilon]\).

**2.** Let \(\{f_n\}\) be a pointwise bounded sequence of complex functions on a countable set \(E\). Prove that there exists a subsequence \(\{f_{n_k}\}\) of \(\{f_n\}\) such that \(\{f_{n_k}(x)\}\) converges for each \(x\in E\).

**3.** Let \(\{f_n\}\) be a uniformly bounded sequence of Riemann integrable functions on \([a,b]\). Let
\[
F_n(x)=\int_a^x f_n(t)\,dt,\qquad a\leq x\leq b.
\]
Prove that there exists a subsequence \(\{F_{n_k}\}\) which converges uniformly on \([a,b]\).

**4.** Let \(E\) be a Lebesgue measurable subset of \([0,1]\), with Lebesgue measure \(m(E)\). If \(0<\alpha<m(E)\), show that there exists a Lebesgue measurable subset \(F\subset E\) such that \(m(F)=\alpha\). (Hint: Examine the function \(f(x)=m(E\cap[0,x])\) on \([0,1]\).)

**5.** Let \(E\subset\mathbb{R}\) be Lebesgue measurable and suppose \(m(E)<\infty\). It is known that for each \(\epsilon>0\) there is a closed set \(F_\epsilon\subset E\) such that \(m(E\setminus F_\epsilon)<\epsilon\). Show that \(F_\epsilon\) can be chosen to be a compact set.

**6.** Let \((X,\Sigma,\mu)\) be a measure space and assume that \(f\) is \(\mu\)-integrable. Prove that for \(\epsilon>0\) there exists a \(\delta>0\) such that \(\left|\int_A f\,d\mu\right|<\epsilon\), whenever \(A\in\Sigma\) and \(\mu(A)<\delta\).
