\begin{examinstructions}
Work the following problems and show all work. Support all statements to the best of your ability.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{0}
\item
Is it true that every continuous map \(f:[0,1]\to[0,1)\) has a fixed point. Justify your answer.
\item
Show that every compact Hausdorff space is regular.
\item
Is it true that
\begin{examsubparts}
\item[\textnormal{(a)}]
every continuous injective map \(f:(0,1)\to\mathbb{R}\) is an embedding?
\item[\textnormal{(b)}]
every continuous injective map \(f:(0,1)\to\mathbb{R}^2\) is an embedding?
\end{examsubparts}
\item
Show that Greek letter theta~\(\Theta\) is not homeomorphic to the figure eight~\(8\). Formally, \(\Theta=S^1\cup[-1,1]\times\{0\}\) and \(8=S_-^1\cup S_+^1\) are subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^2\), where \(S_-^1\) is the unit circle centered at the point \((0,-1)\) and \(S_+^1\) is the unit circle centered at \((0,1)\).
\item
This problem has two parts.
\begin{examsubparts}
\item[\textnormal{(a)}]
Let \(A\subset\mathbb{R}^2\) be a countable set. Prove that \(\mathbb{R}^2\setminus A\) is path connected.
\item[\textnormal{(b)}]
Let \(B\subset\mathbb{R}^3\) be the union of a countable family of lines in \(\mathbb{R}^3\). Prove that \(\mathbb{R}^3\setminus B\) is path connected.
\end{examsubparts}
\end{examproblems}
\begin{examinstructions}
Answer the following with complete definitions or statements or proofs.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{5}
\item
State the Extreme Value Theorem.
\item
State the Tietze Extension Theorem.
\item
Let \(\mathbb{R}_\ell\) denote the reals with the lower limit topology. Is the subspace \(\mathbb{R}_\ell\cap[0,1]\)
\begin{examsubparts}
\item[\textnormal{(a)}]
compact?
\item[\textnormal{(b)}]
connected?
\end{examsubparts}
\item
Is every compact Hausdorff space normal?
\item
Give definition of a locally connected space. Is every path connected space locally connected?
\end{examproblems}
