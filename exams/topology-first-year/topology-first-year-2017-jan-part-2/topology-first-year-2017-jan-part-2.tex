\begin{examinstructions}
Work the following problems and show all work. Support all statements to the best of your ability.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{0}
\item
Show that \(S^1\) is not homeomorphic to \(S^2\).
\item
Let~\(X\) be a connected normal topological space having more than one point. Can~\(X\) be countable?
\item
Prove that homotopy equivalent path connected spaces have isomorphic fundamental groups.
\item
Define retract and deformation retract. Give an example of spaces~\(A\) and~\(X\) such that~\(A\) is a retract of~\(X\) but it is not a deformation retract of~\(X\).
\item
Prove that there is no retraction of the disk onto its boundary.
\end{examproblems}
\begin{examinstructions}
Answer the following with complete definitions or statements or short proofs.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{5}
\item
Can the set of irrational numbers given the standard metric be presented as a countable union of closed subsets?
\item
Define the Stone–Čech compactification and state its characterizing property.
\item
Give an example of a connected space~\(X\) with two points \(x_0\) and \(x_1\) such that \(\pi_1(X,x_0)\) is not isomorphic to \(\pi_1(X,x_1)\).
\item
Compute the fundamental groups \(\pi_1(\mathbb{R}^n \setminus 0)\) for all \(n>1\).
\item
Is the square map \(s:S^1\to S^1\), \(s(z)=z^2\), nullhomotopic?
\end{examproblems}
