\section{Part 1.}
\begin{examinstructions}
Complete Proofs. Support all statements to the best of your ability.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{0}
\item
Let~\(X\) be a topological space. Prove that if~\(X\) is path connected, then~\(X\) is connected.
\item
Let~\(X\) be a topological space, let \(A \subset X\), and let \(B \subset X\). Let \(\overline{A}\) denote the closure of~\(A\). Prove that 
\[
\overline{A \cup B}=\overline{A}\cup\overline{B}.
\]
\item
Let~\(I\) be an arbitrary index set, and let 
\[
X=\prod_{i\in I}X_i.
\]
 Prove that if each \(X_i\) is connected, then~\(X\) is connected. You may use the fact that a finite product of connected spaces is connected, without proving this fact.
\item
Let \((X,d)\) be a metric space. Prove that if~\(X\) is sequentially compact, then~\(X\) is compact.

\par
Recall the definition: \(X\) is sequentially compact if and only if every sequence of points in~\(X\) has a subsequence which converges to a point of~\(X\).
\item
Let \((X,d)\) be a nonempty complete metric space with no isolated points. Prove that~\(X\) is uncountable.

\par
Recall the definition: A point~\(x\) is an isolated point of~\(X\) if and only if the set \(\{x\}\) is open.
\item
Prove that a compact Hausdorff (\(T_2\)) space is normal.
\item
Define an equivalence relation~\(\sim\) on \(X=[0,1]\) by \(x\sim x\) for all~\(x\), \(0\sim 1\), and \(1\sim 0\). Prove that the quotient space \(X/{\sim}\) is homeomorphic to the circle \(S^1\).
\end{examproblems}
