Topology first year exam, January 2026, Part 2

Work the following problems and show all work. Support all statements to the best of your ability.

  1. Show that for every continuous map \(f:S^1\to S^1\) there is \(n\in\mathbb{Z}\) such that \(f\) is homotopic to \(z^n:S^1\to S^1\).

  2. Derive the Brouwer Fixed Point Theorem for \(B^2\) from the Borsuk–Ulam Theorem for \(S^2\).

  3. Show that \(S^2\) is not homeomorphic to \(S^3\).

  4. Show that every map \(f:S^3\to S^1\times S^1\times S^1\) of the 3-sphere to the 3-torus is null-homotopic.

  5. Show that the Stone–Čech compactification of the reals is not metrizable.

Answer the following with complete definitions or statements or proofs.

  1. Compute the fundamental group \(\pi_1(S^2)\).

  2. Show that any two continuous maps \(f,g:X\to I^n\) to the \(n\)-cube are homotopic.

  3. Is the space \(I^I\) separable, where \(I=[0,1]\)?

  4. State the Seifert–van Kampen Theorem.

  5. State the Tychonoff Theorem.

  6. Show that the projective plane \(\mathbb{R}P^2\) is not homeomorphic to the surface of genus 2, \(M_2=T\#T\).

  7. Let \(X=[0,1]^{(0,1)}\) be given the product topology. Is the space \(X\)

    1. (a)

      compact?

    2. (b)

      metrizable?

    3. (c)

      separable?

  8. Let \(X=\mathbb{R}\) and \(A=\mathbb{Z}\) be the reals and the integers respectively. Here \(X^*\) is the partition into \(A\) and singletons.

    1. (a)

      Is the quotient space \(X^*=X/A\) metrizable?

    2. (b)

      What is \(\pi_1(X^*)\)?

  9. Give definition of a deformation retraction.

  10. If \(r:X\to A\) is a retraction, show that the induced map for the fundamental groups \(r_\#\) is surjective.
