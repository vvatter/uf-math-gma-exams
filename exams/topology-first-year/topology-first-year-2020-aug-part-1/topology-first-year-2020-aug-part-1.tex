\begin{examinstructions}
Work the following problems and show all work. Support all statements to the best of your ability.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{0}
\item
Is it true that every continuous map \(f:[0,1]\to(0,1)\) has a fixed point. Justify your answer.
\item
Show that every connected normal space having more than one point is uncountable.
\item
Show that the set of algebraic numbers is countable (A number \(a\in\mathbb R\) is called algebraic if~\(a\) is a root of a polynomial with rational coefficients).
\item
Show that Greek letter theta~\(\Theta\) is not homeomorphic to the Mercedes symbol~\(S\). (Formally, \(\Theta=S^1\cup[-1,1]\times\{0\}\) and \(S=S^1\cup I_1\cup I_2\cup I_3\) are subspaces of \(\mathbb R^2\) where \(S^1\) is the unit circle and \(I_k\), \(k=1,2,3\), are the closed intervals in \(\mathbb R^2\) joining the origin with the points \((0,1)\), \(\left(\frac{\sqrt3}{2},-\frac12\right)\), and \(\left(-\frac{\sqrt3}{2},-\frac12\right)\).)
\item
Let~\(A\) be a proper subset of~\(X\) and let~\(B\) be a proper subset of~\(Y\). If~\(X\) and~\(Y\) are connected, show that \((X\times Y)-(A\times B)\) is connected.
\end{examproblems}
\begin{examinstructions}
Answer the following with complete definitions or statements or proofs.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{5}
\item
State the Extreme Value Theorem.
\item
State the Tietze Extension Theorem.
\item
Is the space \(I^{\mathbb N}\) separable? What about \(I^I\)? Here \(I=[0,1]\) and \(\mathbb N\) is the set of natural numbers.
\item
Prove that the space \(X=\{(x,y)\mid xy=1\}\subset\mathbb R^2\) is locally compact? What is its one-point compactification?
\item
Is every path connected space locally connected?
\end{examproblems}
