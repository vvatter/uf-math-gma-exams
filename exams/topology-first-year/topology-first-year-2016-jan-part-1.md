# Topology, first year exam, January 2016, Part 1

## Part 1.

*Complete Proofs. Support all statements to the best of your ability.*

**1.** Let \(X\) be a topological space. Prove that if \(X\) is path connected, then \(X\) is connected.

**2.** Let \(X\) be a topological space, let \(A \subset X\), and let \(B \subset X\). Let \(\overline{A}\) denote the closure of \(A\). Prove that
\[
\overline{A \cup B}=\overline{A}\cup\overline{B}.
\]

**3.** Let \(I\) be an arbitrary index set, and let
\[
X=\prod_{i\in I}X_i.
\]
Prove that if each \(X_i\) is connected, then \(X\) is connected. You may use the fact that a finite product of connected spaces is connected, without proving this fact.

**4.** Let \((X,d)\) be a metric space. Prove that if \(X\) is sequentially compact, then \(X\) is compact.

Recall the definition: \(X\) is sequentially compact if and only if every sequence of points in \(X\) has a subsequence which converges to a point of \(X\).

**5.** Let \((X,d)\) be a nonempty complete metric space with no isolated points. Prove that \(X\) is uncountable.

Recall the definition: A point \(x\) is an isolated point of \(X\) if and only if the set \(\{x\}\) is open.

**6.** Prove that a compact Hausdorff (\(T_2\)) space is normal.

**7.** Define an equivalence relation \(\sim\) on \(X=[0,1]\) by \(x\sim x\) for all \(x\), \(0\sim 1\), and \(1\sim 0\). Prove that the quotient space \(X/{\sim}\) is homeomorphic to the circle \(S^1\).
