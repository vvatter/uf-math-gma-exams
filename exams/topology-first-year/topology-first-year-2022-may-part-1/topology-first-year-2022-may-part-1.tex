\begin{examinstructions}
Do seven 7 and only seven of the following.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{0}
\item
Let~\(X\) be a set, and let \(\mathcal T\) be the collection of all subsets~\(A\) of~\(X\) such that \(X-A\) is either finite or all of~\(X\).
\begin{examsubparts}
\item[\textnormal{(a)}]
Prove that \((X,\mathcal T)\) is topological space.
\item[\textnormal{(b)}]
Now let \(X=\mathbb R\), the real line.
\begin{examsubparts}
\item[\textnormal{(i)}]
Determine if the sequence \(x_n=7n+8\) converges and if so, to which point or points.
\item[\textnormal{(ii)}]
Show that every infinite set is connected.
\item[\textnormal{(iii)}]
What is \(\overline{\{5\}}\)?
\end{examsubparts}
\end{examsubparts}
\item
Let~\(X\) be a topological space. Let \(D=\{(x,y)\in X\times X:x=y\}\). Prove that~\(X\) is a Hausdorff space if and only if~\(D\) is a closed subset of \(X\times X\), where \(X\times X\) has the product topology.
\item
Show that \(\mathbb R^{\mathbb Z_+}\) with the uniform topology is not connected.
\item
A chain of subsets is a collection \(A_n\) for \(n\in\mathbb Z_+\) with the property that for all~\(i\), \(A_i\cap A_{i+1}\ne\varnothing\). If each \(A_n\) in a chain is connected, show that \(\bigcup_{n\in\mathbb Z_+}A_n\) is also connected.
\item
If~\(A\) is a countable set, show that \(\mathbb R^2-A\) is path connected.
\item
A space is called locally Baire if every point has an open neighborhood that is a Baire space. Prove that locally Baire implies Baire.
\item
Show that the Tietze Extension Theorem implies Urysohn’s Lemma.
\item
Assume that \((X,d)\) is metric. Show the following
\begin{examsubparts}
\item[\textnormal{(a)}]
\(X\) is normal
\item[\textnormal{(b)}]
If~\(X\) is compact then it is second countable
\end{examsubparts}
\end{examproblems}
