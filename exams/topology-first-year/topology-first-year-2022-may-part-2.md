# Topology, first year exam, May 2022, Part 2

*Do seven 7 and only seven of the following. In the following \(D^2\) is the closed, two-dimensional disk, and \(S^1\) is the unit circle, its boundary. The cyclic group of order \(n\), is \(\mathbb{Z}_n=\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}\).*

**1.** Assume that \(X\) is completely regular. Show that \(X\) is connected if and only if \(\beta X\) is connected.

**2.** This problem has three parts.
* a. Define a retract and show there is no retract \(D^2\to S^1\).
* b. Show that any continuous map \(f:D^2\to D^2\) has a fixed point.
* c. If \(A\) is retract of \(D^2\) prove that any continuous map \(f:A\to A\) has a fixed point.

**3.** Let \(M\) and \(N\) be two connected, compact manifolds with points \(x_0\in M\) and \(y_0\in N\). Define an equivalence relation on their disjoint union, \(M\sqcup N\), as \(x_0\sim y_0\) and all other points are just equivalent to themselves. Let \(M\vee N=(M\sqcup N)/{\sim}\). Show that
\[
\pi_1(M\vee N,[x_0])=\pi_1(M,x_0)*\pi_1(N,y_0)
\]
where \([x_0]\) is the equivalence class of \(x_0\).

**4.** Construct a space whose fundamental group is isomorphic to \((\mathbb{Z}\oplus\mathbb{Z}_3)*\mathbb{Z}_5\).

**5.** Show that \(S^2\) is not homeomorphic to \(S^n\) for any \(n>2\).

**7.** The projective plane \(PP\) is constructed by identifying the edges of a disk as pictured below. Compute the fundamental group of \(PP\) as well as that of \(PP\) minus \(n\) points, \(n>0\).

* (a) Define a deformation retract and show it induces an isomorphism on fundamental groups.
* (b) Prove or disprove: a retract induces an isomorphism on fundamental groups.
