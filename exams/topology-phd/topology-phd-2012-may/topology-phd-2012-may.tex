\begin{examinstructions}
Work the following problems and show all work. Support all statements to the best of your ability.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{0}
\item
Does there exist a compact space that contains a discrete infinite subspace?
\item
Let~\(X\) and~\(Y\) be connected spaces and \(X,Y\) is not the one-point space. Show that \(X \times Y \setminus \{z\}\) is connected for any \(z \in X \times Y\).
\item
Prove that \((0,1)\) and \([0,1)\) are not homeomorphic.
\item
Show that the 2-sphere \(S^2\) is not a retract of the projective plane, as well as the projective plane is not a retract of \(S^2\).
\item
Let \(GL(n)\) be the space of all invertible real \(n \times n\)-matrices. IS \(GL_n\) is compact? connected?
\end{examproblems}
\begin{examinstructions}
Answer the following with complete definitions or statements or short proofs.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{5}
\item
Explain why every map \(S^n \to S^1\), \(n>1\) is null-homotopic.
\item
State the Lefschetz Fixed Point Theorem. Explain that every continuous map \(D^n \to D^n\) has a fixed point.
\item
State the Mayer–Vietoris sequence for homology.
\item
Show that the spaces \(S^2 \times S^2\) and \(S^2 \vee S^2 \vee S^4\) are not homotopy equivalent.
\item
Does every function \(f:\mathbb{N}\to\mathbb{R}\) admits a continuous extension \(\bar f:\beta\mathbb{N}\to\mathbb{R}\) of the Stone–Čech compactification?
\item
Formulate the Invariance of Domain Theorem.
\item
Let~\(M\) be a closed 4-dimensional simply connected manifold. What can you say about \(H_3(M)\)?
\item
Does there exist a cover space of the figure eight that has the non-trivial abelian fundamental group?
\item
Let~\(X\) be a compact Hausdorff topological space. Describe all compact subsets of the space \(C(X,\mathbb{R})\) of continuous functions \(X\to\mathbb{R}\) in uniform topology.
\item
State the Universal Coefficient Theorem for Cohomology.
\end{examproblems}
