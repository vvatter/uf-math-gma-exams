\begin{examinstructions}
Work the following problems and show all work. Support all statements to the best of your ability.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{0}
\item
Compute \(\pi_1(T^2)\) and \(H_*(T^2)\), where \(T^2 = S^1 \times S^1\) is the 2-dimensional torus.
\item
Let~\(X\) be a connected completely regular topological space having more than one point. Can~\(X\) be countable?
\item
Prove that there is no map of degree 3 from \(S^2\) to the torus \(T^2\).
\item
Does there exist a covering space of the 2-dimensional sphere with four points deleted with nontrivial abelian fundamental group?
\item
Show that the 2-dimensional sphere with four points deleted cannot be a topological group.
\end{examproblems}
\begin{examinstructions}
Answer the following with complete definitions or statements or short proofs.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{5}
\item
State the Baire Category Theorem.
\item
State the homology Mayer–Vietoris Theorem.
\item
State the Urysohn Lemma.
\item
Do the following short exact sequences always split 
\[
0 \to A \to B \to \mathbb{Z}^2 \to 0
\]
 
\[
0 \to \mathbb{Z} \to A \to B \to 0\ ?
\]
\item
Compute the Euler characteristic \(\chi(\mathbb{R}P^2 \times S^1 \times S^2 \times S^3)\).
\item
Give an example of \(A \subset X\) such that~\(A\) is a retract of~\(X\) but not a deformation retract.
\item
State the Lefschetz Fixed Point Theorem.
\item
Can irrational numbers be presented as a countable union of closed in~\(\mathbb{R}\) subsets?
\item
Draw a picture of the universal cover of the 2-sphere with the segment joining the north and south poles.
\item
List all~\(i\) for which there is a closed orientable 6-manifold~\(M\) with \(H_i(M) = \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z} \oplus \mathbb{Z}\).
\end{examproblems}
