\begin{examinstructions}
Work the following problems and show all work. Support all statements to the best of your ability.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{0}
\item
Show that \(\pi_1(S^2)\) is trivial.
\item
Show that there is no retraction of the Möbius band onto its boundary.
\item
Does there exists a covering space of the figure eight with nontrivial abelian fundamental group?
\item
Show that the 2-dimensional sphere with three points deleted cannot be a topological group.
\item
Let~\(M\) be a 4-dimensional closed simply connected manifold. Show that every continuous map \(f:M\to M\) which is homotopic to the identity has a fixed point.
\end{examproblems}
\begin{examinstructions}
Answer the following with complete definitions or statements or short proofs.
\end{examinstructions}
\begin{examproblems}{5}
\item
State the Baire Category Theorem.
\item
State the Lefschetz Fixed Point Theorem.
\item
State the Jordan Curve Theorem.
\item
State the Five Lemma.
\item
Show that no covering of the 2-torus has the homotopy type of a figure eight.
\item
Let \(S_r(x,y)\) denote a circle of radius~\(r\) centered at \((x,y)\in\mathbb{R}^2\). Let \(\Sigma R\) be the suspension over Hawaiian ring \(R=\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}S_{1/n}(1/n,0)\). Is \(\Sigma R\)
\begin{examsubparts}
\item[\textnormal{(a)}]
locally path connected?
\item[\textnormal{(b)}]
locally simply connected?
\item[\textnormal{(c)}]
semi-locally simply connected?
\end{examsubparts}
\item
State the Tietze Extension Theorem.
\item
Define retraction and deformation retraction.
\item
State the Universal Coefficient formula for homology.
\item
Let~\(X\) be a complete metric space such that no point is isolated. Can~\(X\) be countable?
\end{examproblems}
